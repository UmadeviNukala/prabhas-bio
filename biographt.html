Career

Early Success: Prabhas gained recognition for roles in films like "Chatrapati". "Baahubali" and Pan-Indian Star Status: His portrayal of Baahubali and his son Sivudu in the "Baahubali" films propelled him to global stardom, making him a pan-Indian star. Breaking Records: He became the first Indian actor to have multiple films with a worldwide gross opening of over ₹100 crore and the first to have a film gross over ₹1000 crore worldwide. Versatility and Dedication: Prabhas is known for his dedication to his roles and his willingness to explore diverse genres, as seen in his work in "Saaho," "Radhe Shyam," and "Salaar". International Recognition: His success has extended beyond India, with a strong fan following in countries like Russia and Japan. "Salaar" and Continued Success: Prabhas has continued to achieve success with films like "Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire," which became the highest-grossing Telugu film of 2023. Future Projects: He is currently working on "Kalki 2898 AD," a film inspired by the Hindu epic Mahabharata, and has upcoming projects including "The Raja Saab" and "Fauji" Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju ([pɾabʱaːs]; born 23 October 1979), known mononymously as Prabhas, is an Indian actor who predominantly works in Telugu cinema.[4] One of the highest-paid actors in Indian cinema, he is known as Rebel Star in Telugu cinema. He is one of the most commercially successful Telugu actors of all time and has been featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list since 2015.[9] Referred to in the media as "Rebel Star", he has a significant fan following and has received seven Filmfare Awards nominations, a Nandi Award, and a SIIMA Award.[10][11] Prabhas made his acting debut with the drama Eeswar (2002), and later attained his breakthrough with the action romance Varsham (2004). He went on to act in other commercially successful films such as Chatrapathi (2005), Bujjigadu (2008), Billa (2009), Darling (2010), Mr. Perfect (2011), and Mirchi (2013), winning the Nandi Award for Best Actor for his performance in the lattermost.[12][13] Prabhas then starred in a dual role in the blockbuster epic action duology Baahubali: The Beginning (2015) and its sequel Baahubali 2: The Conclusion (2017), with the latter emerging as the highest-grossing Indian film of all time at that point. Prabhas became the first pan-Indian star and earned international recognition for his performance in the films.[14][15][16][17] He has since starred in the action thriller Saaho (2019), the action drama Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire (2023), and the science fiction film Kalki

Achievements

First South Indian Actor at Madame Tussauds: Prabhas became the first South Indian actor to have a wax statue at the Madame Tussauds museum in Bangkok. Multiple 100 Crore Openings: He holds the record for having multiple films with a worldwide gross opening exceeding ₹100 crore. Pan-Indian Star Status: His success transcends regional boundaries, making him a pan-Indian superstar. Iconic Actor: He is recognized as an icon, with several awards and nominations recognizing his contributions to the film industry. Highest Grossing Telugu Film (2023): "Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire" emerged as the highest-grossing Telugu film of 2023.
