Career

Prabhas, whose full name is Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju, is a renowned Indian actor predominantly known for his work in Telugu cinema. He made his debut with the film *Eeswar* in 2002, but it was the 2004 romantic action film *Varsham* that marked his first major success. He continued to build his career with commercial hits like *Chatrapathi* (2005), *Billa* (2009), *Darling* (2010), *Mr. Perfect* (2011), and *Mirchi* (2013), for which he received the prestigious Nandi Award for Best Actor. Prabhas achieved unprecedented fame with the role of Amarendra Baahubali and Mahendra Baahubali in S. S. Rajamouli’s *Baahubali* franchise. *Baahubali: The Beginning* (2015) and its sequel *Baahubali 2: The Conclusion* (2017) became monumental successes, with the latter becoming the highest-grossing Indian film of its time, earning over ₹1000 crore globally and transforming Prabhas into a pan-Indian superstar. Following *Baahubali*, he starred in high-budget films like *Saaho* (2019), which showcased his action persona, and *Radhe Shyam* (2022), where he explored a romantic role. His 2023 release *Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire* emerged as the highest-grossing Telugu film of the year, further proving his box office strength. Prabhas is widely praised for his dedication, spending years on single projects and physically transforming himself for demanding roles. Internationally, he has a massive fan base, especially in countries like Japan and Russia. Currently, he is working on *Kalki 2898 AD*, a science fiction epic based on the Hindu mythology of Mahabharata, alongside upcoming projects like *The Raja Saab* and *Fauji*. With his versatile acting, humble personality, and consistent box office performance, Prabhas has firmly established himself as one of the most influential and highest-paid actors in Indian cinema.

Achievements

Prabhas has earned a distinguished place in Indian cinema through a combination of box office records, critical acclaim, and immense fan following. One of his most iconic achievements is becoming the first South Indian actor to be immortalized with a wax statue at Madame Tussauds Bangkok, reflecting his global appeal. With the Baahubali series, Prabhas shattered long-standing records—Baahubali 2: The Conclusion not only became the highest-grossing Indian film at the time of its release, collecting over ₹1800 crore worldwide, but also became the first Indian film to gross ₹1000 crore globally, setting a new industry benchmark. His commitment to Baahubali was unprecedented, as he dedicated nearly five years to the two-part epic, turning down multiple projects during that time. Prabhas has consistently delivered ₹100 crore+ opening films, a rare feat for Indian actors, and was among the first Telugu actors to achieve true pan-Indian stardom, drawing massive audiences in Hindi, Tamil, Malayalam, and Kannada-speaking regions. His 2023 release, Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire, became the highest-grossing Telugu film of the year, adding another major commercial hit to his filmography. He is a recipient of the Nandi Award for Best Actor for Mirchi and has earned seven Filmfare Awards nominations, along with a SIIMA Award, underscoring his consistent recognition by critics and peers. Prabhas has also made his mark as one of the highest-paid actors in India, often praised for his dedication, discipline, and down-to-earth personality. In addition to his cinematic achievements, his growing popularity in countries like Japan, Russia, and the UAE further cements his status as an international star. His influence extends beyond the screen, with his style, humility, and philanthropic nature winning hearts across generations.

Goals

Prabhas, known for his dedication and humility, has set clear goals that reflect both his personal growth and his commitment to the film industry. One of his primary ambitions is to continue delivering commercially successful and critically acclaimed films that appeal to diverse audiences across India and abroad. As a versatile actor, he constantly strives to explore new genres and challenging roles, refusing to be confined to one particular image or style. His recent choices—from mythological epics like Kalki 2898 AD to intense action dramas like Salaar—showcase his eagerness to push artistic boundaries. Prabhas also aims to expand his global fan base and take Indian cinema to new international heights, especially as his popularity in countries like Japan, Russia, and the Middle East continues to grow. Beyond acting, he is deeply committed to making a positive impact on the film industry, supporting innovation in storytelling, encouraging new talent, and contributing to high-quality cinema. With upcoming projects like The Raja Saab and Fauji, Prabhas continues to focus on reinventing himself while staying grounded, ensuring that his journey remains both impactful and inspirational.
